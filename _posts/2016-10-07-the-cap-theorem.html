This theorem suggests that any distributed system built, it is possible to simultaneously provide the only two of the following three assurances -
a) Consistency - Commits are atomic across the entire distributed system. Every read provides the most recent write or an error.
b) Availability - The system remains accessible and operational at all times. Every request receives a response regardless of whether it is the most recent information or not.
c) Partition Tolerance - The system continues to operate despite arbitrary partitioning due to network failures. It takes a complete network failure to cause system to respond incorrectly.

ACID and BASE represent two design philosophies at opposite ends of the consistency-availability spectrum. The ACID properties focus on consistency and are the traditional approach of databases. BASE was created in the late 1990s to capture the emerging design approaches for high availability and to make explicit both the choice and the spectrum. Modern large-scale wide-area systems, including the cloud, use a mix of both approaches. BASE stands for Basically Available, Soft state, Eventually consistent.  Soft state and eventual consistency are techniques that work well in the presence of partitions and thus promote availability.

I am of an opinion that beating the CAP theorem is to adopt to Eventual consistency and focus on Availability and partition tolerance.
